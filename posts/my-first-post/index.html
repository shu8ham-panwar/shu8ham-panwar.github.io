Tesla’s “$0 Marketing Budget” - Why Tesla Pays $0 for Their Marketing.

Introduction

Tesla Motors has no advertising, no ad agency, no CMO, no dealer network. Yet they have managed to create a household name and buzz in the market amongst millions of car lovers.

Tell a story that resonates with individuals.

Sharing a story, a vision and a mission is one of the most powerful ways to turn your customers into ambassadors.

This interface could also be used to remind customers of Tesla’s mission:

"Accelerate the world’s transition to sustainable energy."

Emphasize Value Over Cost.

Instead of concentrating on what the consumers spend, show them what the value they receive will help people equate charging with an even more positive experience in the current sense, putting more attention to the benefits (fuel savings, environmental benefits). On Tesla’s website, this “savings” marketing tactic is ubiquitous, so why not see it here is shocking.

Word-Of-Mouth Starts With A Great Product.

Elon Musk confirmed that word-of-mouth is Tesla’s #1 source of growth and that it’s mainly because of the quality of their products.

Tesla is famous for its Net Promoter Score (NPS) of 963. In comparison, BMW’s NPS is “good”, but barely hits 40.

With 650,000+ Teslas on the road, making it easier for fans to spread the word can massively improve Tesla’s referral rates and sales.

Opportunity Signals

A simple Google search for tesla savings spreadsheet…returns 350,000 results.

This is a strong signal that this experiment would address users' needs while improving their overall experience.

Lessons for companies from Tesla.

This is the hardest thing to do, and it is at the very heart of marketing as well. Starting by having something extremely desirable to a significant market is the best way to express value. If the value is self-evident, the communication component is relatively trivial.

The “huge sector” in Elon Musk’s eyes is humanity itself; electric cars are valuable because they are the future of sustainable transport. They are unbelievably hard to work on, too.
